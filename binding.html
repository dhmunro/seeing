UNDER CONSTRUCTION

Binding the Universe

From planetary motion to universal gravitation

Equal and opposite reaction

Newton's third law of motion - for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction - means there is no such thing as an isolated force. Forces always appear in equal and opposite pairs, so if the Earth pulls you down, you must be pulling the Earth up with equal force. Similarly, if the Sun pulls the Earth toward it, the Earth must equally pull on the Sun. But you know that the Earth pulls on both you and the Moon - so perhaps it is not surprising that it pulls on the Sun as well. The effect of this counter-force would be imperceptible for the same reason your pull on the Earth is imperceptible: The mass of the Sun must be huge compared to the mass of the Earth.

The force of gravity at the surface of the Earth has the unusual feature that it causes all objects, no matter what size or material, to accelerate downward at the same rate. Kepler's laws have the same unusual feature - the acceleration is the same for any planet or moon at the same distance from its central body. Apparently, this is a property of gravity in general. From his second law of motion, F = ma, Newton knew this meant that the force of gravity must be directly proportional to the mass of the object being attracted. Since the objects must be attracting each other equally, the gravitational force between them must be proportional to both their masses.

Apparently, Newton reasoned, between any two objects of masses m and M separated by a distance r, there is a gravitational force directly proportional to both masses and inversely proportional to the square of the separation, GMm/r² - universal gravitation - where G is a universal constant. In one sense, this is a dramatic simplification - all manner of phenomena are described by this single rule. In another sense, universal gravitation vastly complicates your world. Instead of a limited rule like Kepler's laws of planetary motion, Newton tells you that if you really want to understand the solar system, you have to account for the forces between every rock and drop of fluid in the whole thing! The force between any pair of objects may be simple, but the number of interactions has skyrocketed.

Note that the gravitational acceleration is proportional to the mass of the attracting body. That is, Kepler's cube-square law constant a³/T² or Newton's inverse square law constant uL = GM, are proportional to, and therefore measures of, the mass of the central body! For example, because a³/T² is a thousand times smaller for the moons orbiting Jupiter than for the planets orbiting the Sun, Newton deduced that the mass of Jupiter is a thousand times less than the mass of the Sun. And Earth is 330 times less massive than Jupiter. This is still how we know the masses of all astronomical objects: You need to find something orbiting the object and measure that orbital size and period (or at least how much the object deflects something passing nearby); then, like Newton, you can compute the mass of the central object compared to the mass of the Sun.
